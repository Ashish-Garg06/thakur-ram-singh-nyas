The Sacred Legacy of Thakur Ram Singh Ji

Thakur Ram Singh Ji stands as the eternal spiritual and moral inspiration behind the vision of this Trust. His extraordinary life reflected complete dedication to discipline, service, humility, cultural preservation, and राष्ट्र निर्माण. Every initiative undertaken by the Trust draws strength from the ideals he embodied throughout his life.

Born on 16 February 1915 in Jhandavi village of Hamirpur district, Himachal Pradesh, he displayed exceptional academic brilliance from an early age. While pursuing M.A. in History at FC College, Lahore, he topped the university examinations in 1942 and was awarded a gold medal. Despite being offered a prestigious lectureship by the college principal, he chose the higher path of selfless national service and dedicated his life entirely as a Pracharak of the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh.

Beginning his organizational journey in 1942, he served tirelessly across Kangra, Amritsar, Punjab, Assam, and the northeastern regions of Bharat. Under the guidance of revered leaders such as Shri Madhavrao Mule, Shri Balasaheb Deoras, Shri Guruji Golwalkar, and Moropant Pingle, he became one of the most respected and disciplined Pracharaks of his era.

“A life devoted completely to Dharma, Nation, Society, and the preservation of Bharat’s civilizational memory.”

Thakur Ram Singh Ji played a historic role in strengthening the Akhil Bharatiya Itihas Sankalan Yojana and later became its national president. His mission was to awaken Bharat to its authentic civilizational history and intellectual heritage. Through seminars, research initiatives, scholarly guidance, and institution-building, he inspired generations of historians, thinkers, and young workers across the nation.

His efforts directly inspired the establishment of the Thakur Jagdev Chand Memorial Research Institute at Neri, Hamirpur, where several major historical and cultural projects continue to flourish today.

Even in advanced age and declining health, his dedication never weakened. Till the final days of his earthly journey, he remained deeply engaged in historical research, national thought, and organizational guidance. Those who met him witnessed a rare combination of simplicity, intellectual depth, unwavering discipline, and spiritual strength.

For us, he is not merely the founder of an institution — he is the living source of its values, character, and purpose. His life continues to illuminate our path and inspire every effort towards service, education, cultural pride, and social harmony.
