The Sacred Legacy of Thakur Ram Singh Ji

Thakur Ram Singh Ji stands as the eternal spiritual and moral inspiration behind the vision of this Trust. His extraordinary life reflected complete dedication to discipline, service, humility, cultural preservation, and राष्ट्र निर्माण.

Born on 16 February 1915 in Jhandavi village of Hamirpur district, Himachal Pradesh, he displayed exceptional academic brilliance from an early age. While pursuing M.A. in History at FC College, Lahore, he topped the university examinations in 1942 and was awarded a gold medal. Despite being offered a prestigious lectureship, he chose the higher path of selfless national service as a Pracharak of the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh.

Beginning his organizational journey in 1942, he served tirelessly across Kangra, Amritsar, Punjab, Assam, and the northeastern regions of Bharat — under the guidance of Shri Madhavrao Mule, Shri Balasaheb Deoras, Shri Guruji Golwalkar, and Moropant Pingle.

"A life devoted completely to Dharma, Nation, Society, and the preservation of Bharat's civilizational memory."

He played a historic role in strengthening the Akhil Bharatiya Itihas Sankalan Yojana and later became its national president — inspiring scholars across Bharat to rediscover authentic Indian history from a civilizational perspective.

His efforts directly inspired the establishment of the Thakur Jagdev Chand Memorial Research Institute at Neri, Hamirpur, where several major historical and cultural projects continue today.

Legacy & Contribution

A Life Dedicated to Bharat and Society

01
Scholarship Rooted in Service
Though academically brilliant and awarded a gold medal in History, he chose not a life of comfort or prestige, but a life of sacrifice, discipline, and national service as an RSS Pracharak.
02
Architect of Historical Awakening
Through Akhil Bharatiya Itihas Sankalan Yojana, he inspired scholars across Bharat to rediscover, document, and preserve authentic Indian history from a civilizational perspective.
03
Nationwide Organizational Leadership
From Punjab to Assam and the northeastern regions, he spent decades strengthening organizational work, mentoring workers, and nurturing a spirit of disciplined service.
04
Lifelong Dedication Until His Final Days
Even during illness and advanced age, his thoughts remained focused on Bharat's history, research projects, and future generations. His commitment never diminished.
05
Eternal Source of Inspiration
The Trust carries forward his ideals of seva, humility, scholarship, discipline, and national consciousness. Every activity undertaken here is a humble tribute to his sacred legacy.
